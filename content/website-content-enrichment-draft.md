## 2. Safe public-facing content angles for beeswax product pages
The following content is based on common commercial positioning patterns found across public beeswax supplier and product pages. It is intentionally conservative and should be treated as marketing / inquiry-support copy, not as hard technical claims.

### A. General beeswax category explanation
Beeswax is commonly supplied in yellow and white forms, and in different handling formats such as blocks, pellets, and flakes. Buyers often choose between these options based on application, appearance needs, dosing convenience, and downstream production workflow.

### B. Yellow vs white beeswax
#### Yellow beeswax
- keeps a more natural color tone
- often positioned for candles, polish, furniture care, and some cosmetic formulations
- suitable for buyers who want a natural wax presentation

#### White beeswax
- positioned as a more refined-looking option
- often considered for personal care, specialty blends, and projects that require a cleaner appearance
- suitable when buyers care about lighter color presentation in the finished product

### C. Blocks vs pellets / flakes
#### Block / slab form
- suitable for buyers comfortable with bulk handling
- often used in traditional bulk supply and wholesale shipment discussions
- may fit buyers whose downstream process already includes cutting or melting workflow

#### Pellets / flakes
- easier for dosing, batching, and blending
- convenient for manufacturers and formulation teams
- often preferred where handling speed and cleaner production workflow matter

### D. Common application categories buyers care about
- cosmetics and personal care
- candle making
- furniture care and polish
- food-related project discussion depending on market requirements
- pharmaceutical or specialty applications depending on project context
- industrial processing and wax blending

### E. Common B2B buyer questions
- Do you supply yellow and white beeswax?
- Can you provide pellets or flakes?
- What packaging options can be discussed?
- Is sample support available before bulk order?
- What information is needed for quotation?
- Can documents or basic specification information be discussed during inquiry?
